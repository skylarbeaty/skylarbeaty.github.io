Projects

️️What have I been up to?

Phase Drift

An arcade shooter, designed around mobile friendly controls, where you play on new stages every run. The development of this was very iterative and based on player feedback on the mechanics, weapons, enemies, and so on.

It’s available free on Android and iOS. I also made a video about how its procedural stage generation works

Procedural Generation

I’ve created several different methods of procedurally generating levels, including custom solutions for my own projects, like I did for Phase Drift. I’ve also recreated methods from popular games, like Spelunky and The Binding of Isaac, in order to have a template for making tutorials on the subject.

The code for these can be found on my GitHub page, and the tutorials are on my Youtube page.

Other work

I have many different projects from game-jams, hackathons, coursework, and personal projects. Help two birds find their way through a hedge maze in Two in a Bush. Stack up ridiculously large sandwiches in Disaster Deli. See my games on my Itch page, and other work on my GitHub.

About Me

️️The Basics

I’m William Beaty, a programmer and game designer who hails from Cleveland. I’m studying computer science at Kent State University with a concentration in Game Programming. Although my focus is in programming and design, as an independent developer I find myself wearing many hats, from drawing up graphical assets to creating sound effects.

I got a taste for level design as a child playing excitebike, and played many more games with level editors throughout my childhood. Making these levels was more fun for me than actually playing the normal game, and when I got other people to play them, they came alive. When I was about twelve I realized I could actually make my owns games, and I never looked back.

I love to share what I know with others, and so I’ve created several tutorial videos on youtube. I play guitar and ukulele, and I love music. It’s hard to catch me programming without listening to music. One of my bigger interests is space flight and I’ve been keeping up with recent launches and watching live when I can, as well learning a lot about the technology involved.
